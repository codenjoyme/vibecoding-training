# Choosing Prompt Language & Freewriting

**Duration:** 5-7 minutes  
**Skill:** Pick the prompt language that maximizes your throughput (usually your native one) and use freewriting to produce long, raw prompts faster than your inner critic can stop you

**👉 [Start hands-on walkthrough](walkthrough.md)**

## Topics

- The real bottleneck in GenAI work: not tokens, but your reading/writing/sleeping bandwidth
- Why prompting in your native language is almost always cheaper than English (mental tokens vs LLM tokens)
- When token economics actually outweigh native-language speed (the 2× vs 50× rule)
- Freewriting: DDoS your inner critic by writing without backspace and without grammar
- Modern LLMs read scrambled, typo-ridden text fine — stop polishing prompts as you write
- Separating prompt language from generated artifact language (prompt in your tongue, code/docs in English)
- Using freewriting in pair sessions, GTD-style brain dumps, and morning pages

## Learning Outcome

Decide consciously which language you write prompts in, and switch to freewriting mode when you need to produce a long prompt fast — without arguing with yourself about word choice, grammar, or typos.

## When to Use

- You catch yourself spending more time polishing a prompt than the model spends answering it
- You're stuck staring at an empty chat — you know what you want but can't start writing
- You're pair-prompting with someone and watch them edit grammar instead of expressing intent
- You need to dump a complex problem on the AI and don't want to lose nuance to "tidy" prose
- You're debating whether to switch your prompts from your native language to English to "save tokens"
