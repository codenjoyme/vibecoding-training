# IDE Workspace as Knowledge Base

**Duration:** 15 minutes

**Skill:** Use your IDE workspace as a lightweight knowledge base — organize documents, meeting notes, and scattered information into a folder and query them through AI chat instead of building a full RAG pipeline.

**👉 [Start hands-on walkthrough](walkthrough.md)**

## Topics

- Workspace as a poor man's RAG: why it works and when it's enough
- Organizing unstructured information into a queryable folder
- Asking AI to analyze, summarize, and cross-reference documents
- Summarizing session outcomes into reusable instructions or knowledge files
- Security and confidentiality: what gets sent to AI servers and how to get approval

## Learning Outcome

You can take any pile of documents — project specs, meeting transcriptions, Slack threads, regulatory texts — drop them into an IDE workspace, and use AI chat to extract answers, find contradictions, build summaries, and make decisions faster than reading everything yourself.

### Required Skills & Tools

- VS Code or Cursor with AI assistant (GitHub Copilot or equivalent)

## When to Use

- A new project lands on your desk and comes with 20+ documents
- You're onboarding to a new team or domain and drowning in context
- You have meeting transcriptions that need action items extracted
- You received a regulatory or compliance document and need to understand impacts
- You want to compare multiple proposals or technical solutions side by side
- You need to prepare a summary or decision brief from scattered sources
- Building a full RAG system is overkill for a one-off analysis task
